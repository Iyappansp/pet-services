10 Signs Your Dog is Absolutely Thriving (And How GPS Walks Help)

👩‍⚕️
Jessica Mills
Co-Founder & CEO
June 1, 2025 5 min read

As a dog parent, it can be hard to know if your pup is truly happy and healthy — especially on the days you're away. Here are 10 clear signs your dog is absolutely thriving, and how regular GPS-tracked walks play a crucial role.

1. Bright, Shiny Eyes

A happy, healthy dog has clear, bright eyes with no discharge or cloudiness. Dull or watery eyes can signal illness or stress — so keep an eye out (pun intended).

2. Good Appetite

Dogs that eat consistently and enthusiastically are usually in great health. Sudden changes in appetite — either increase or decrease — can be early warning signs worth noting.

3. Regular, Healthy Sleep

Adult dogs sleep 12–14 hours a day on average. A dog that gets enough exercise during their daily walks will sleep deeply and contentedly. GPS tracking helps confirm your dog actually covered meaningful ground.

4. Playful, Energetic Behavior

A dog that initiates play, wags its tail frequently, and bounces around with enthusiasm is a happy dog. Dogs that are under-exercised or anxious tend to be either lethargic or destructive.

5. Healthy Weight and Muscle Tone

You should be able to feel (but not prominently see) your dog's ribs. Regular walks help maintain a healthy weight and build muscle tone — both key indicators of overall wellbeing.

6–10. More Signs to Watch For

  • Regular bathroom habits: Consistent, healthy bathroom breaks signal a well-functioning digestive system.
  • Healthy coat: A shiny, soft coat without excessive shedding is a great sign of nutrition and low stress.
  • Social behavior: A thriving dog enjoys greeting people and other animals rather than hiding or showing fear.
  • Good breath (relatively!): Seriously foul breath can indicate dental issues. Clean teeth = happier, healthier pup.
  • Physical affection: Dogs that seek out cuddles and physical contact are feeling safe, loved, and content.
